Some water in between

02/11/2019

Almost three years later, I’m sitting in the place where I first saw him. A cozy but crowded café, immersed in the noise of the espresso machines working non-stop, helping to quiet my thoughts, like it did back then.

He was not tall and had dark, black hair, with a thick, perfectly shaped beard, but none of that I noticed first. I saw his eyes, childish and playful. As he got closer, I saw the brown of his irises, the hint of gold and honey, long lashes framing them and brushing the air. I could see a spark when the skin around those eyes wrinkled and his full lips curved into a smile to show his perfect teeth. He was suddenly standing in front of me and nothing would ever be the same. That much I knew. 

As our millennial generation often do, we met online, on a weekend when I was visiting Copenhagen, and we started texting when I was already on my way back home to Aarhus, two hundred miles away and some water in between. We should have stopped there, but we didn’t, and soon it was too late. 

Irene, 28
From Colombia. Lived in Copenhagen for a few years and then moved to Aarhus for a job as an acoustic engineer in the wind power industry.

Sergio, 29
(Argentinian flag) (Danish flag) Spontaneous? Yes! Salsa dancing? Yes! Red wine? Yes!

(That’s as much as I can remember from his profile, particularly because I love to dance, but I’m not the spontaneous type and red wine gives me headaches).  

He had just come back from a month in Colombia and we found common ground right away. I think he stopped reading after seeing the word “Colombia” in my profile and didn’t realize for a couple of days that we actually didn’t live in the same city. Still, we exchanged messages for two or three weeks, probably thinking that we could just be friends, have someone to speak the same language with and share our similar cultures. But during those first weeks, and without really knowing how or when, the relationship transitioned into something else. We would text every day first thing in the morning, right before falling asleep at night, and every hour in between. He was so quick to tell me everything about him, while I was taking everything in. We flirted, we joked, we shared, and the tone of the messages started changing and becoming closer, more affectionate, more caring, more intimate, even though we were never very explicit about it. Less than a month later I was back in Copenhagen to see him in person. 

I waited for him in my favorite cafe, with my eyes glued to my book, going over and over the same page, while my head was somewhere else fighting to stay calm. My hands were cold, even when I wrapped them around a cup of hot coffee, and my heart was beating faster in anticipation. I pretended not to see him through the window when he first approached the entrance, but I saw him, and I followed him all the way in, my book still keeping me safe. 

He saw me and we knew each other right away. We embraced and it was like meeting an old friend. He sat and told me about his day while I finished my drink. Casually. Like old friends do.

Later on, we went for a long walk in the cold and windy air of the Danish winter and immersed ourselves in deep conversation. He talked. A lot and fast, in his melodic Argentinian accent. Oh how I loved that accent. He had a cold and his voice was raspy. He looked sick and tired, his eyes red, but he never mentioned it and we stayed out late, finishing the night with a beer (I can’t stand beer, but I didn’t care). I was mesmerized, enchanted, and listened. A lot and slow. But most of all, we laughed. Belly-aching, eyes-tearing laugh. Our sense of humor was so easy and in tune. 

I left early the next morning, back to my life two hundred miles away, leaving behind what I thought had ended with a good night hug. But didn’t.  

Today, almost three years later, sitting in that cafe where I first saw him, I remember those two years. That’s how long we were together. That’s how long we were apart. Two years of memories and so many of them are scattered around this city. Walking around these past days during my weekend visit, everything reminded me of him. Of us. Everything we gained and lost. Everything that was and now isn’t. Him. Me. Us. And some water in between.
